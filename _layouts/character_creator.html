Character Sheet

Doing Things

When you do something challenging the GM will tell you to roll and add a Skill (such as Strength or Cunning). You can either use the "Roll Dice" button, or roll a ten-sided die and map the result using the table below.

Roll Result
10 Skill + 2
9, 8 Skill + 1
7, 6, 5, 4 Skill + 0
3, 2 Skill - 1
1 Skill - 2

Success and Failure

The GM will determine if you've succeeded based on how hard the task was and how high you rolled.

Difficulty Description
+0 Average
+1 Proficient
+2 Professional
+3 Expert
+4 Master
+5 Olympic

Advantage and Disadvantage

When the GM tells you to take Advantage, you roll twice and take the better result. Similarly, Disadvantage has you roll twice and take the worse result.

Health

Health is measured in Boxes. Whenever you take damage, you mark a box. The GM will tell you if the box is Physical or Mental depending on the source of the damage.

If you run out of Physical or Mental boxes, you become incapacitated. You cannot act in the game until at least one of those boxes is cleared, either by resting or healing.

Healing

The primary way to recover boxes is Making Camp. Some characters also have abilities that can recover Physical boxes.

Offense

When you attack something, you roll a Skill just like you would for any other action. Using a sword might be Strength or Agility. Casting a spell would be Magic. Shooting a gun could be Perception. You successfully hit an enemy if your roll beats their Defense.

If you hit, the target takes a box of damage. Certain abilities and situations might change this to two boxes, change the type of damage from Physical to Mental, or produce effects in addition to damage.

Defense

When you try to avoid harm, you roll a Skill. Blocking an attack might use Strength. Dodging could use Agility. If you fail to avoid harm, you mark a box of damage.

Turn Order

Turn order is decided at the beginning of some (but not all) conflicts. The GM will tell you to roll Agility to determine when you act.

Kits

{% include example_kit.html %}

Kits are powerful items that you've been trained to use. They let you cast magic, perform alchemy, or wield enchanted weapons. The kits that you have access to are determined by your Specialization.

At any given time, you can equip a maximum of four kits.

Changing Kits

You can change Kits whenever you Make Camp. You unlock new kits as your character becomes more experienced.

Main Types of Kits

  • Gear swords: Weapons, armor, and other equipment.
  • Talismans auto_fix: Artifacts that channel magical energy into spells.
  • Alchemy science: Products of alchemic innovation, ranging from explosives to poisons.
Other Types of Kits
  • High Damage shift: Weapons that deal two boxes of Physical damage, instead of one.
  • Accurate point_scan: Weapons that add a +1 to attack rolls, making attacks much more likely to land.
  • Reaction replay: Kits that trigger on specific circumstances.

Resources

Potions

The Alchemist's resource, used to heal two boxes of Physical damage or empower Alchemy kits.

Fortune
The Soldier's resource, used to reroll dice or empower Gear kits.
Mana
The Mage's resource, used to empower Talisman kits or combine Talismans for complex spells.
Tricks
The Scoundrel's resource, used for many things:
  • Amplify an Alchemy, Gear, or Talisman kit.
  • Declare I Know a Guy, describing a local contact you might plausibly have that owes you a favor.
  • Declare that you have What You Needed, describing an item or object that can help you with the current situation.

Advancement

You grow your character in two ways:

Skill Points
Skill Level Upgrade Skill Point Cost
0 -> 1 1
1 -> 2 2
2 -> 3 3
3 -> 4 4

One Skill Point is awarded at the beginning of every session. They can be spent to increase Skills, with higher level Skills being more difficult to raise.

Tiers

Tiers are larger increases in power that represent the number of kits you have access to. New characters have access to six kits, while more experienced characters may have twelve, eighteen, or twenty-four kits.

Tier level increases when the party achieves a significant milestone. The GM will tell you when this happens.
