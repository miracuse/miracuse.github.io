Character Sheet


Skills

Skills are what you're good at. The more points you have in a Skill, the better outcomes you'll have when using it.

Doing Things

Outcome = Skill Level + Roll Result

When you do something challenging, the GM will tell you to roll a ten-sided die. You'll convert the roll using the list below, then add it to your Skill.

Roll Result
10 +2
8, 9 +1
4, 5, 6, 7 +0
2, 3 -1
1 -2

Success and Failure

The GM will determine if you've succeeded based on how hard the task was and how high you rolled.

Difficulty Description
+0 Easy
+1 Challenging
+2 Professional
+3 Expert
+4 Master
+5 Olympic

Advantage and Disadvantage

When the GM tells you to take Advantage, you roll twice and take the better result. Similarly, Disadvantage has you roll twice and take the worse result.

Health

Health is measured in Boxes. Whenever you take damage, mark a Box in either the Physical or Mental health tracks.

If you run out of boxes (Physical or Mental), you become incapacitated. You cannot act in the game until at least one of those boxes is cleared, either by resting or healing.

Making Camp

Boxes are recovered by resting at camp. Some characters have Kits that can heal Physical boxes. No Kits exist to heal Mental boxes.

While resting at camp, you can also swap out your Kits.

Pushing It

You can sacrifice a Mental Health Box to recover a Resource (Charge Point, Fortune Point, Mana Point, Prep Point). Be careful, it's possible to to incapacitate yourself by doing this.

Offense

When you attack something, you roll a Skill. Hitting something with a sword might be Strength or Agility. Attacking with a spell would be Magic. Shooting with a bow or gun could be Perception. You successfully hit an enemy if your roll is higher than their Defense.

If you hit, the target takes a Box of damage. Certain Kits and situations might change this to two Boxes, deal Mental damage instead of Physical, or produce effects in addition to damage.

Defense

When you try to avoid harm, you roll a Skill. Blocking an attack might use Strength. Dodging could use Agility. If you fail to avoid harm, you mark a Box of damage. You successfully defend against against harm if your roll is equal to or greater than the enemy's Offense. 

Note: Enemies do not roll any dice.
Their Offense and Defense are flat values that you roll against.

Turn Order

Turn order is decided at the beginning of some (but not all) conflicts. The GM will tell you to roll Agility to determine when you can act.

Kits

Kits are special abilities you have. They let you cast magic, perform alchemy, or do advanced martial techniques. The Kits you have access to depend on your Class and Specialization.

Changing Kits

You can swap out your equipped Kits whenever you make camp. You can have four Kits equipped at one time.

Acquiring New Kits

You start with six Kits available based on your character's Specialization. When your character has spent enough time adventuring, they'll unlock a second Specialization with six more Kits to choose from.

The number of Kits you can equip at one time will always be four.

Resources

Resources are pools of power that you can spend to briefly supercharge Kits.

Charge Points

The Alchemist's resource, able to supercharge kits with the "Alchemy" tag. Can also be used to:

  • Produce a Potion to heal two Boxes of Physical damage or treat afflictions like poison.
  • Produce a Grenade to inflict destruction in an area-of-effect.
  • Pull out What You Needed, bringing out an item that you might plausibly have that can help you with your current situation.
Fortune Points

The Soldier's resource, able to supercharge kits with the "Technique" tag. Can also be used to:

  • Reroll any roll, a maximum of one time.
  • Escape Harm by sheer luck or skill, avoiding damage.

Mana Points

The Mage's resource, able to supercharge kits with the "Spirit" tag. Has no other uses.

Prep Points

The Scoundrel's resource, able to supercharge kits with any tag. Can also be used to:

  • Produce a Disguise to infiltrate, confuse, or assume an alias.
  • Disappear from Sight even in the middle of combat.
  • Declare I Know a Guy, describing a contact you might plausibly have that owes you a favor.
  • Use a Magic Device to use a Skill other than Magic with a "Spirit" Kit.
