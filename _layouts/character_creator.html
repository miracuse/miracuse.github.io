Character Sheet


Doing Things

When you do something challenging the GM will tell you to roll and add a Skill (such as Strength or Cunning). You can either use the "Roll Dice" button, or roll a ten-sided die and map the result using the table below.

Roll Result
10 Skill + 2
9, 8 Skill + 1
7, 6, 5, 4 Skill + 0
3, 2 Skill - 1
1 Skill - 2

Success and Failure

The GM will determine if you've succeeded based on how hard the task was and how high you rolled.

Difficulty Description
+0 Average
+1 Proficient
+2 Professional
+3 Expert
+4 Master
+5 Olympic

Advantage and Disadvantage

When the GM tells you to take Advantage, you roll twice and take the better result. Similarly, Disadvantage has you roll twice and take the worse result.

Health

Health is measured in Boxes. Whenever you take damage, you mark a box. The GM will tell you if the box is Physical or Mental depending on the source of the damage.

If you run out of Physical or Mental boxes, you become incapacitated. You cannot act in the game until at least one of those boxes is cleared, either by resting or healing.

Healing

The primary way to recover boxes is Making Camp. Some characters also have abilities that can recover Physical boxes.

Offense

When you attack something, you roll a Skill just like you would for any other action.

Using a sword might be Strength or Agility. Casting a spell would be Magic. Shooting with a bow or gun could be Perception. You successfully hit an enemy if your roll beats their Defense.

If you hit, the target takes a box of damage. Certain abilities and situations might change this to two boxes, change the type of damage from Physical to Mental, or produce effects in addition to damage.

Defense

When you try to avoid harm, you roll a Skill. Blocking an attack might use Strength. Dodging could use Agility. If you fail to avoid harm, you mark a box of damage.

Turn Order

Turn order is decided at the beginning of some (but not all) conflicts. The GM will tell you to roll Agility to determine when you act.

Kits

Kits are powerful items you prepare each day. They let you cast magic, practice alchemy, or wield enchanted weapons. At any given time you have access to four kits determined by your class and specialization.

Changing Kits

You can change Kits whenever you Make Camp. You unlock new kits as your character becomes more experienced.

Types of Kits

  • Weapons: Powerful equipment that adds additional effects to attacks.
  • Talismans: Artifacts that channel magical energy into spells.
  • Alchemy: Products of alchemic innovation, ranging from explosives to poisons.

Resources

Resources are caches of power that let you use special abilities and amplify your equipped kits. Like health, they are measured in boxes and recover when you Make Camp.

Charge Points

The Alchemist's resource, in the form of especially potent ingredients or a stocked pack. Can be used to:

  • Amplify an Alchemy Kit Empower an equipped alchemy kit to go far beyond its usual usage.
  • Produce a Potion to heal two Boxes of Physical damage or treat afflictions like poison.
  • Produce a Grenade to inflict destruction in an area-of-effect.
  • Use What You Needed, creating or bringing out an item that you might plausibly have that can help you with your current situation.
Fortune Points

The Soldier's resource, in the form of unnatural luck and extreme skill. Can be used to:

  • Amplify a Weapon Kit Empower an equipped Weapon to go far beyond its usual usage.
  • Reroll any roll, a maximum of one time.
  • Escape Harm by sheer luck or skill, avoiding damage.

Mana Points

The Mage's resource, in the form of debts owed by spirits or reservoirs of inner strength. Can be used to:

  • Amplify a Talisman Empower an equipped Talisman to go far beyond its usual usage.
  • Combine Talismans Combine two Talismans to produce a more complex effect.

Prep Points

The Scoundrel's resource, in the form of single-use items and contingency plans. Can be used to:

  • Produce a Disguise to infiltrate, confuse, or assume an alias.
  • Disappear from Sight even in the middle of combat.
  • Use a Magic Device to use Agility or Cunning instead of Magic when using a Talisman.
  • Declare I Know a Guy, describing a local contact you might plausibly have that owes you a favor.
