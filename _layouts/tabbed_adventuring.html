Adventuring

Combat

Turn Order

Combat is any situation where several characters need to take different actions and turn order matters. The most common scenario involves fighting, but other possibilities might include fixing a sinking ship or racing a rival adventuring party for treasure.

  • Roll Agility to determine the order that characters will act.
  • On their turn, characters can take one action (usually a single roll of the dice).

Actions

In general, a player's turn ends after they roll dice or do something time consuming. For example:

  • Swinging a sword
  • Drinking an elixir
  • Casting a spell
  • Intimidating an enemy

Actions and turn order rules aren't set in stone. There are plenty of things that won't take up an entire action, like saying a few words to another person. There are also times when it makes sense for a character to react outside of their turn, like stepping into oncoming fire to protect an ally.Ultimately, a player's turn ends when it makes narrative sense. The GM will provide guidance as necessary.

Understanding Damage

Normally, a successful attack by a Player causes one box of Physical damage. There are a variety of situations where this can change though. Some Kits add additional damage to an attack, or deal Mental damage, or have other effects. Likewise enemies may have their own special abilities which modify the basic "one attack / one damage" result.

These effects are discussed in more detail under the Tactics tab for this section.

Exploration

Scouting

Players looking to explore dangerous areas will need to be quiet and observant. The following Skills apply:

  • Cunning: Determines how stealthy the scout is (no roll), and whether or not they detect any danger (roll).
  • Perception: Determines what information is gained about the area.
  • Focus: Determines what magical effects are present in the area, if any.

There and Back

By and large, Scouting takes place over a single action. The player character quietly makes their way to the area being explored, takes note of anything interesting, and reports back to their team.

Lengthier expeditions might be conducted if the party agrees to it, but remember that spending too much time solo is dangerous, and not terribly fun for the other players.

Camping

Making camp gives characters the opportunity to recover and change their Kits. However, resting in dangerous areas should be accompanied by a watch to prevent ambushes.

  • Two characters roll Perception and take the lowest result

Changing Kits

Characters can swap out their current Kits for new ones provided the following is true:

  • They're somewhere safe (like a city or in camp)
  • They have at least one full hour of time

All boxes are cleared at this time, including Alchemist Uses per Kit, Soldier Fortune Points, and Scoundrel Prep Points.

Persuasion

Anytime you want to convince a non-player character of something, you'll need to roll an appropriate Skill. Each class has their own approach to persuasion with some being better suited to particular situations.

Skill Use
Knowledge Debate, rhetorical skill, and logic.
Bravery Challenge, intimidate, and impress.
Aura Awe, command, and speaking with spirits.
Cunning Deceit, fast talking, and working with animals.

Teamwork

Players can help one another with actions provided that there's some narrative justification for doing so. The player being helped receives Advantage on the relevant roll.

Player 1: I want to bend the metal bars on the prison door open.

GM: Okay, roll Physique.

Player 2: Wait. Can I help them?

GM: Do you have any points in Physique?

Player 2: No...

Player 3: I do.

GM: Alright, Player 3 can help. Player 1, roll with Advantage.

Tactics

Players are encouraged to take creative approaches when facing challenges in-game. This section outlines a few suggestions.

Modified Attacks

A successful attack causes the target to mark a single box of damage. However, many Kits can change this behavior. A few examples are listed below, though clever players may find combinations not on this list by exploring their options.

Type Examples Description
Standard Sword, Arrow, Bullet, Spirit of Fire One Box of Physical Damage
Area of Effect Grenade, Hordebreaker, Fireball Spell One Box of Physical Damage vs. Many Targets
Mental Intimidate/Provoke, Arta Valar, Spirit of Sound One Box of Mental Damage
High Damage Iron Strength, Attack+Poison, Assassinate Two Boxes of Physical Damage
Attack w/ Effect Swordbreaker, Arta Tuco One Box of Physical Damage + Effect
Attack w/ Hazard Thermal Charges, Trenchsteel Charges, Fireball Spell One Box of Physical Damage + Environmental Hazard

Using the Environment

Your surroundings can be the key to overcoming a difficult challenge. Think about how you can leverage them.

  • Creating Hazards and Obstacles, by setting things on fire or destroying architecture
  • Recruiting NPC's, by rallying a crowd to your cause or using "I Know a Guy"
  • Acquiring helpful items, through negotiation or theft
  • Strategizing Kits with party members, such as mixing Trenchsteel Charges and Spirit of Force
