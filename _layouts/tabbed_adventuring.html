Adventuring

Skirmish

A skirmish is any situation where several characters need to take different actions and turn order matters. The most common scenario is combat, but other possibilities might include fixing a sinking ship or racing a rival adventuring party for treasure.

  • Roll Agility to determine the order that characters will act.
  • On their turn, characters can take one action (usually a single roll of the dice).

Actions

In general, a player's turn ends after they roll dice or do something time consuming. For example:

  • Swinging a sword
  • Drinking an elixir
  • Casting a spell
  • Intimidating an enemy

Actions and turn order rules aren't set in stone. There are plenty of things that won't take up an entire action, like saying a few words to another person. There are also times when it makes sense for a character to react outside of their turn, like stepping into oncoming fire to protect an ally.Ultimately, a player's turn ends when it makes narrative sense. The GM will provide guidance as necessary.

Combat

Combat is a common type of skirmish where the objective involves defeating enemies, typically by inflicting harm.

Using Skills to Cause Harm

Skill Description
Perception Shooting with a crossbow or other precise weapon
Physique Using a hefty weapon, such as a sword or longbow
Agility Using a swift weapon, such as throwing knives or an epee
Aura Using magic to cause harm

Using Skills to Avoid Harm

Skill Description
Agility Dodging out of the way, deflecting attacks
Physique Enduring, pushing through difficulty or pain
Bravery Overcoming fear, exhaustion, or attacks on the mind

Persuasion

Anytime you want to convince a non-player character of something, you'll need to roll an appropriate Skill. Each class has their own approach to persuasion with some being better suited to particular situations.

Skill Use
Knowledge Debate, rhetorical skill, and logic.
Bravery Challenge, intimidate, and impress.
Aura Awe, command, and speaking with spirits.
Cunning Deceit, fast talking, and working with animals.

Scouting

Players looking to explore dangerous areas will need to be quiet and observant. The following Skills apply:

  • Use the lowest Cunning of everyone participating, no roll required
  • Roll Perception and take the highest result of everyone participating
  • In case of magical effects, roll Focus and take the highest result of everyone participating

Better Cunning results will ensure that the scouts are not discovered, while better Perception and Focus rolls will reveal additional information about the surroundings.

There and Back

By and large, Scouting takes place over a single action. The player character quietly makes their way to the area being explored, takes note of anything interesting, and reports back to their team.

Lengthier expeditions might be conducted if the party agrees to it, but remember that spending too much time solo is dangerous, and not terribly fun for the other players.

Camping

Making camp gives characters the opportunity to recover and change their Kits. However, resting in dangerous areas should be accompanied by a watch to prevent ambushes.

  • Two characters roll Perception and take the lowest result

Changing Kits

Characters can swap out their current Kits for new ones provided the following is true:

  • They're somewhere safe (like a city or in camp)
  • They have at least one full hour of time

All boxes are cleared at this time, including Alchemist Uses per Kit, Soldier Fortune Points, and Scoundrel Prep Points.

Teamwork

Players can help one another with actions provided that there's some narrative justification for doing so. The player being helped receives Advantage on the relevant roll.

Player 1: I want to bend the metal bars on the prison door open.

GM: Okay, roll Physique.

Player 2: Wait. Can I help them?

GM: Do you have any points in Physique?

Player 2: No...

Player 3: I do.

GM: Alright, Player 3 can help. Player 1, roll with Advantage.
