The Alchemist

Uses Per Rest

Each Alchemist Kit has a limited number of uses based on the character's Knowledge. Alchemists mark a box each time they use a specific Kit, and clear all boxes for all Kits when resting at camp.

What You Needed

In the event that a character needs some item that might be unusual for them to carry (such as a bag of ball bearings), they can roll Knowledge to see whether or not they thought ahead and prepared that item ahead of time.

Sharing Items

Drugs and Tools can be safely shared with other party members. Bombs and Poisons require untrained users to roll Knowledge to use safely.

The Soldier

Fortune Points

Soldiers have a number of Fortune Points equal to their Bravery. Fortune Points can be used to reroll any action the Soldier takes and take the better result.

  • Fortune Points can only be used once per roll
  • Fortune Points can be recovered by resting or through certain Kits
  • Fortune Points can be used for any action where the player rolls, not just for combat

Morale

Soldiers also have the ability to influence the morale of friends and foes using Bravery.

  • Intimidation can force enemies to make distance or retreat.
  • Provoking enemies will incite them to ignore others and focus on the Soldier.
  • Rallying allies grants them Advantage on their next roll in combat.

The Mage

Cantrips

The player chooses a spirit, describes an effect they want to achieve, and rolls Aura to see how well it goes. Using a Mage Kit this way is called casting a Cantrip.

Spellcasting

Mages can combine two of their Kits to produce stronger and more complex effects. Using Kits this way is called spellcasting, and requires the caster to mark damage for one of their Mental Health boxes. Having a higher Focus allows Mages to cast more spells in a single day.

The Scoundrel

Stealth

Scoundrels rarely attract notice unless they want to. By default, anyone not actively searching an area must have a higher Perception than the Scoundrel's Cunning to detect them.

Additionally, Stealth does not require a dice roll. Scoundrels are assumed to be hidden unless they're discovered, or someone is actively looking for them.

Sneak Attacks

Attacking while in Stealth counts as an automatic 10, providing a +2 to the attack without a roll.

I Know a Guy

A player can declare that his character Knows a Guy with some particular talent or knowledge that can help the party. The player describes a new NPC, giving them a name and short background, before rolling Cunning to see how well-aquatinted their character is with the NPC.
