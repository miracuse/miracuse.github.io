What You Needed

Inventory management handled in narrative fashion. Instead of tracking each individual item on their person, characters are assumed to be carrying whatever might be reasonable given their class and background.

In the event that a character needs some item that might be unusual for them to carry (such as a bag of ball bearings), they can roll Knowledge to see whether or not they thought ahead and prepared that item ahead of time.

The Soldier

Fortune Points

Soldiers have a number of Fortune Points equal to two times their Bravery. Fortune Points can be used to reroll any action the Soldier takes.

  • Fortune Points can only be used once per roll
  • Fortune Points reset after resting
  • Fortune Points can be used for any action where the player rolls, not just for combat

Morale

In addition to being resilient against mind-affecting attacks, Soldiers can exert control over the morale of friend and foes.

They can spend their action to Provoke or Intimidate enemies using Bravery, convincing them to either focus their attacks on the Soldier or retreat altogether. Alternatively, Soldiers can Rally allies using the same approach, providing Advantage on their next roll.

The Mage

Cantrips

Mage Kits are open ended in their use. The player chooses a spirit, describes an effect they want to achieve, and rolls Aura to see how well it goes. Using a Mage Kit this way is called casting a Cantrip. Two examples are shown below, though more can be found on the Interactive Game Sheets

Cantrip Description
Firebolt Spirit of Fire; do Physical damage to an enemy by throwing fire.
Torch Spirit of Fire; create a small flame above your palm that sheds light.

Spellcasting

Mages can combine two of their Kits to produce stronger and more complex effects. Using Kits in this fashion is called spellcasting, and requires the caster to mark damage for one of their Mental Health boxes. Having a higher Focus allows Mages to cast more spells in a single day.

Spell Description
Fireball Spirit of Fire + Air; an area-of-effect Physical attack that sets the surroundings on fire.
Stone Wall Spirit of Earth + Warding; creates a wall of rock to serve as a barricade.

The Scoundrel

Stealth

Scoundrels rarely attract notice unless they want to. By default, anyone not actively searching an area must have a higher Perception than the Scoundrel's Cunning to detect them.

Unlike many other applications of Skills, Stealth does not require a dice roll. The player character is assumed to be hidden unless they take a noticeable action, such as attacking an enemy.

Sneak Attacks

Attacking while in Stealth counts as an automatic roll of 10, requiring no roll and dealing the maximum damage possible.

Sneak attacks can be a powerful tool, but players should note that performing one removes their character from Stealth. Unfortunate Scoundrels may find that their ambush has left them exposed to retribution.

I Know a Guy

A player can declare that his character Knows a Guy with some particular knowledge or role that can help the party. The player describes a new NPC, giving them a name and short background, before rolling Cunning to see how well-aquatinted their character is with the NPC.

High rolls translate into a friendly relationship, while low rolls may mean that the player character is on bad terms with them.
