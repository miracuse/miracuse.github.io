The Mage

The Mage

The Mage forms pacts with the primal spirits of the world to perform miracles and spells. Some come by their magic through careful study, others through natural inclination or religious tradition. All Mages, regardless of their background, are marked by their dealings with spirits and become less human as they grow in power.

  • Skills: Magic, Focus
  • Subclasses: Priest, Shaman, Wizard
Equipment and Appearance
* A staff, wand, book, or other object used in your spellcasting
* An unnatural physical characteristic, such as red irises or glowing tattoos
* Clothing that marks you as a Mage, such as rune-embroidered robes or vestments of faith
* A voice that commands attention in some way, such as a gravelly rasp or tendency to rhyme

Magic Basics

Magic Kits

Mage Kits are deliberately open-ended, allowing players creative freedom to experiment with effects. A player might take Spirit of Flame and use it for:

  • Throwing fire at enemies.
  • Making lights to help see while exploring in the dark.
  • Creating a fire to keep warm during a snowstorm.
  • Any other fire-related purpose.

Cantrips and Spellcasting

Cantrips are the default way to use a Mage Kit. The player describes an effect they want to achieve using their Kit and roll Magic to see how well it goes.

Spellcasting is a more powerful, but more costly, way to use magic. Mages combine two different Spirit Kits to produce stronger and more complex effects. Like Cantrips, the Mage rolls Magic to determine how successful the attempt was. They must also mark a point of Mental Health damage afterwards, reflecting the strain of using powerful magic.

Mage Role

In combat...

The Mage unleashes unpredictable havoc on the enemy; striking foes blind, sending bolts of lighting across the battlefield, or casting any number of other impossibilities.

Socially...

The Mage speaks with supernatural charm. Weak-willed listeners find themselves hypnotically compelled by the sound of a Mage's voice, while those with sterner minds react with immediate distrust.

While exploring...

The Mage may call on their spirits to aid in finding hidden locations or communicating with the inhabitants of a foreign land. Their sixth sense can be helpful when dangerous magic is in the air.
