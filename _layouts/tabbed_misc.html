Custom Classes

The four Classes can be used for many character concepts, but Players may also choose to modify their starting Skills to better reflect a particular character concept. For example, a Paladin might mix a Soldier's Skills with a Mage's Kits to create a character that can use magic and fight with weapons.

Creating a Custom Class

  • Choose one Subclass (i.e. Priest, Bard) from any of the Classes
  • Choose one of the following Skill distributions
    • Standard (Two Skills at +2)
    • Versatile (One Skill at +2, Two Skills at +1)

Example: Paladin

Subclass:
  • Mage/Priest
Skills:
  • Strength +2
  • Bravery +1
  • Magic +1
Description:

A holy knight trained in the arts of war and magic. Paladins aren't well-suited to spellcasting, but gain a great deal of versatility from their access to Priest Kits. Likewise, Paladins have none of the martial Kits of a Soldier, but have similar durability and Strength.

Healing

Although Making Camp is the primary way to recover from damage and injuries, other options exist for certain Subclasses.

Skill Use

  • (Alchemist) Elixir
  • (Soldier) Hospitaler
  • (Mage) Spirit of Restoration
When using these Kits, roll the associated Skill (such as Magic or Knowledge). The result determines either how many boxes of damage are cleared or whether or not a narrative outcome was achieved.
  • Restoring Physical damage
  • Treating an affliction (poison, disease, etc.)
  • Reviving someone from incapacitation

Note: The Alchemist's Elixir and the Scoundrel's Herbalist are the only Kits that can recover Mental health.
