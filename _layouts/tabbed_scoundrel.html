The Scoundrel

The Scoundrel

The Scoundrel is a master of misdirection. They blend in with crowds and shadows, only revealing themselves when the time is right. Many employ their talents as hunters or merchants, though it's just as common to see them working as thieves and assassins.

  • Skills: Cunning, Agility
  • Kits: Criminal, Hunter, Performer
Equipment and Appearance
* An easily hidden melee weapon, such as a dagger or garrote
* An unassuming ranged weapon, such as throwing knives or a shortbow
* Inconspicuous clothes for traveling, and fashionable dress for formal affairs
* Lockpicks, wire cutters, and other thieves' tools hidden on your person
In combat...

The Scoundrel waits for the right opportunity to dispatch an enemy, showing a preference for dangerous targets like mages. Once exposed they might make a hasty getaway, or throw themselves into the fight with a frenzy of quick strikes.

Socially...

The Scoundrel bewilders and confuses with fast-talk. Perhaps because of their ability to present an friendly face, many also have an affinity for working with animals. Listeners that prefer a straightforward response will quickly tire of the Scoundrel's evasive speech.

While exploring...

The Scoundrel is nearly always the scout of a party. They move unseen through dangerous areas, ever ready to book a fast retreat should danger arise.

Prep Points can be used for a variety of purposes:

  • Change a Kit without setting up camp
  • Use I Know a Guy
  • Use What You Needed

I Know a Guy

A Scoundrel can declare that his character Knows a Guy by expending a Prep Point. The player describes a new non-playable character with some particular talent or knowledge that can help the party. The player should provide a name, description, and an explanation of how their character knows this person.

What You Needed

In the event that a Scoundrel needs some item that might be unusual for them to carry, they can use What You Needed by expending a Prep Point.

Stealth

Stealth and Sneak Attacks

By default, anyone not actively searching an area must have a higher Perception than the Scoundrel's Cunning to detect them.

Attacking while in Stealth counts as an automatic 10, providing a +2 to the attack without a roll.
