The Soldier

The Soldier

The Soldier is a warrior without peer. They may be knights, bandits, infantrymen, or come from other walks of life. What makes one a Soldier is not their profession but their skill at arms, courage, and a surprising amount of luck.

  • Skills: Strength, Bravery
  • Subclasses: Fencer, Knight, Myrmidon
Equipment and Appearance
* A melee weapon such as a sword, spear, or bayonet
* A ranged weapon such as a longbow, musket, or flintlocks
* Armor ranging from thick leathers to metal plates
* A sign of rank, storied weapon, or other marking of prowess

Fortune

Fortune Points

Soldiers have a number of Fortune Points equal to two times their Bravery. Fortune Points can be used to reroll any action the Soldier takes.

More Details

  • Fortune Points can only be used once per roll.
  • Fortune Points can only be used once per action in combat.
  • Fortune Points can be used for any action where the player rolls, not just for combat.
  • Players take the result of the second roll, even if the result is worse.
  • Fortune Points can be recovered by resting or through certain Kits.

Soldier Role

In combat...

The Soldier is often at the frontlines, terrorizing enemies and bringing ruin to those that approach. Their Strength makes them dangerous and hardy opponents.

Socially...

The Soldier is an imposing figure. They carry an air of Bravery that impresses like-minded persons and cows others. Less militant audiences may be put off by their demeanor, seeing them as brutish and curt.

While exploring...

The Soldier provides raw muscle, breaking down obstacles by sheer force. Their abundant luck has saved many a party from unexpected traps and ambushes.

Techniques

{% include example_kit.html %}

Techniques are Kits that modify attacks. They add additional effects, such as increasing damage, hitting multiple enemies, or targeting multiple enemies at once.

In addition to their passive effect, Techniques can also be amplified or mixed together by expending Fortune Points. Two examples are provided on this page, but the effects are based on what the Player is trying to accomplish rather than a preset list of outcomes.

  • Cleave (Amplified): Greatly expand the area-of-effect of your attack.
  • Cleave + Sunder: Make an area-of-effect attack that deals two boxes of Physical damage to all enemies and objects in range.
