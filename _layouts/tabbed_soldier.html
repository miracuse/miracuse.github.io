The Soldier

The Soldier

The Soldier is a warrior without peer. They may be knights, bandits, infantrymen, or come from other walks of life. What makes one a Soldier is not their profession but their skill at arms, courage, and a surprising amount of luck.

  • Skills: Physique, Bravery
  • Kits: Fortune, Discipline, Valor
Equipment and Appearance
* A melee weapon such as a sword, spear, or bayonet
* A ranged weapon such as a longbow, musket, or flintlocks
* Armor ranging from thick leathers to metal plates
* A sign of rank, storied weapon, or other marking of prowess
In combat...

The Soldier is often at the frontlines, terrorizing enemies and bringing ruin to those that approach. They may serve as a vanguard preventing foes from reaching more fragile members of the party.

Socially...

The Soldier is an imposing figure. They carry an air of self assurance that impresses like-minded persons and cows others. Less militant audiences may be put off by their demeanor, seeing them as brutish and curt.

While exploring...

The Soldier provides raw muscle, breaking down obstacles by sheer force. Their abundant luck has saved many a party from unexpected traps and ambushes.

Soldier Mechanics

Fortune Points

Soldiers have a number of Fortune Points equal to their Bravery. Fortune Points can be used to reroll any action the Soldier takes and take the better result.

  • Fortune Points can only be used once per roll
  • Fortune Points can be recovered by resting or through certain Kits
  • Fortune Points can be used for any action where the player rolls, not just for combat

Morale

Soldiers also have the ability to influence the morale of friends and foes using Bravery.

  • Intimidation can force enemies to make distance or retreat.
  • Provoking enemies will incite them to ignore others and focus on the Soldier.
  • Rallying allies grants them Advantage on their next roll in combat.
